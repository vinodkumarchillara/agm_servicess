HARDNESS TESTING

Magnetic Particle Testing (MT or MPI) is a non-destructive testing (NDT) method used to identify surface and near-surface defects in ferromagnetic materials. It involves magnetizing the material and then applying ferromagnetic particles, which will be attracted to areas where the magnetic field is disrupted by a discontinuity. This technique is widely used in metallurgical services to ensure the quality and safety of various components.

Magnetic Particle Testing is used in various metallurgical applications, including: Crack detection in welds: Identifying cracks in welds of pressure vessels, pipelines, and other structures. Inspection of castings and forgings: Detecting internal flaws like porosity, inclusions, and shrinkage cavities. Detection of fatigue cracks: Identifying cracks that develop due to repeated stress cycles, especially in rotating or moving parts. Quality control in manufacturing: Ensuring the integrity of components during various stages of production.
